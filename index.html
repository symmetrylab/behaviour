Are your price targets systematically skewed?

The Positive/Negative Bias module measures an analyst's median upside — the gap between price target and current price — and ranks it against the peer universe. Analysts at the extremes are flagged, and the system shows whether their optimism (or pessimism) translates into actual outperformance.

How well-calibrated are your prediction ranges?

The Overconfidence module measures what percentage of actual 12-month returns fall within an analyst's top-of-band to bottom-of-band range. A well-calibrated analyst should capture ~65% of outcomes. Below that signals range-setting that is far too narrow — a textbook marker of overconfidence.

Are your analysts acting on new information?

The Status-Quo Bias module tracks how frequently an analyst submits updates and changes their recommendation, compared to peers covering the same stocks. Low-frequency analysts miss mid-cycle alpha — the system simulates the return impact of more timely re-ratings.
